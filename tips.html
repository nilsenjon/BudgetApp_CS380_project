How to build your budget:

  • Gather all of your financial statements and calculate your monthly average.
  • Record all of your sources of income. If you have a variable income, use the income from the lowest-earning month as a baseline for the rest of your budget.
  • Compile a list of your monthly expenses and break them up into two categories: fixed and variable.
    • Variable expenses are the expenses that change from month to month, like groceries, gas, entertainment, and eating out.
    • Fixed expenses are relatively the same each month, like mortgage/rent, car payments, cable, and internet.
  • Add up your monthly income and monthly expenses and see how they compare.
    • If you have more income than expenses, this is a good thing and will allow you to allocate the excess funds to other areas of your budget.
    • If you have more expenses than income, begin looking at your variable expenses and take money away from those items first.
  • Make adjustments to your monthly expenses in your budget, ensuring that you account for all of your income when allocating money to different categories.

Other tips to consider while building a budget:

  • Always start with the most important categories first, then move down the list to the less important categories.
  • Ensure that your budget has a buffer to allow you to put aside small amounts of money throughout the month for any unexpected expenses.
  • Consider using the 50-20-30 approach.
    • 50% goes to necessities, like housing payments and food.
    • 20% goes to paying debts or increasing savings.
    • 30% goes to whatever you want!

Maintaining your budget:

  • It's okay to make mistakes. Forgive yourself when you make them and remember to stay focused on the goal. It can take time to adjust to a budget.
  • Curious how an expense will impact your budget? Add it on your home page and see the change in your pie chart. Then you can simply remove the expense and your finances will revert to their original state.
  • Share your budget with other members of your household and make sure they are on the same page as you. Consider also creating the budget together to account for all expenses and incomes.
  • Adjust your budget as things change and remember to save money on the good months.
  • Keep tracking your progress and see how you're approaching your goals.
  • Use cash on the categories that you consistently struggle with.
  • Be nice to yourself.
    • It's okay to make mistakes. Forgive yourself when you do and remember what your goals are.
    • Don't compare your financial situation to others' around you. Focus on yourself and your family and what is right for you.
  • Stay focused on your goals to maintain your motivation.

Adjust your habits:

  • Start paying with cash instead of credit. It's easier to prevent overspending when you are required to count out each dollar of a purchase.
  • When eating out, consider splitting entrees or only ordering appetizers.
  • Make your own gifts for birthdays and holidays. DIY something special rather than buying something new and expensive.
  • Buy used items whenever possible. Go thrift shopping instead of buying clothes at the mall. Check out listings on websites like Craigslist instead of buying new furniture from a department store.
  • Try meal prepping to limit the amount you spend on eating out.

Make some financial changes:

  • Consider changing your phone plan to a cheaper package.
  • Consider changing your cable package to a bundle that combines your cable and internet. This will save you money with most service providers.
  • Consider dropping the cable package and using a cheaper streaming service, like Netlfix or Hulu.
  • Cancel any unnecessary subscriptions and turn off auto-renew for all remaining subscriptions.
  • Consider selling old items that you don't use anymore or set up a trade to exchange them for something that you would use more often.

Additional tips:

  • Write your grocery list before going grocery shopping and utlize as many coupons as you can.
  • Sign up for loyalty programs to further maximize your savings when grocery shopping.
  • Designate one day a week for grocery shopping and stay away from the store on the remaining days.
  • Count your coins and cash every day and collect all of it throughout the month to deposit into your savings.
  • Take advantage of discounts on entertainment. Cheaper movies on Tuesdays? Free days at the museum? Student discounts for tickets? Pay attention to discounts to allow yourself to have fun while sticking to your budget.
  • Pay attention to annual sale periods when looking to make a major purchase. Remember to time this purchases and never make them impulsively.
  • Restrict your online shopping and never save your credit card information. If you need to type in your card information with each purchase, you'll likely make fewer impulse purchases.
  • Monitor your energy usage and make the proper changes to decrease your monthly electric bill.
  • Set a savings goal that is both specific and realistic.

Q: How should I format my .CSV files in order to ensure they are properly handled?

  • The format for the .CSV files should match the order in which you input expenses on the Home page, separated by commas. Each expense should take up one line, and each line should consist of a description, a value, and a date. An example of a properly formatted line in a .CSV file is: Movie tickets, $32.50, 05/23/2020.
